One of the main reasons users avoid screen guards is the fear of tempered glass touch lag and reduced display clarity. Nobody wants to use a premium HDR screen if it looks dim and hazy.

Cheap screen guards use low-grade soda-lime glass and thick adhesive layers, which can reduce touch sensitivity and cause glare. 

Premium aluminosilicate glass protectors use a microscopic electroplated oleophobic coating and high-grade optical adhesive. This ensures zero touch lag, maintaining smooth scrolling and crystal-clear display transparency.
